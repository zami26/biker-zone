Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet every 5–10 years or after an impact. Check for cracks, dents, or damaged foam. Sun exposure and wear reduce effectiveness. Follow manufacturer recommendations. Safety first!

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Check tires for cracks or low pressure, brakes for functionality, chain lubrication, and frame for rust or damage. Test ride cautiously to ensure everything works properly before regular use.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Obey traffic laws, use hand signals, ride with traffic, wear a helmet, stay visible with lights, avoid distractions, and always yield to pedestrians. Stay alert and ride safely!
